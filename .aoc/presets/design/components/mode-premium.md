Use this mode when the goal is premium frontend direction or a redesign pass that must feel less generic.

Prioritize:
- stronger hierarchy before decoration
- cleaner spacing rhythm and grouping
- less template-looking layout patterns
- more deliberate typography and surface treatment
- complete states: loading, empty, error, success, hover, focus, active
- motion with purpose, restraint, and reduced-motion fallbacks
- performance-safe implementation choices

Do not force a visual trend for its own sake.
Do not overwrite explicit brand constraints.
Do not invent dependencies that are not in the project.

Push for:
- distinctive but usable layouts
- premium polish without bloat
- component/state completeness
- mobile-safe fallbacks for asymmetric desktop composition
- transforms/opacity-first motion
- fewer generic equal-card grids unless they are clearly the right choice

When useful, recommend:
- `design-premium-ui` for high-end frontend generation guidance
- `design-redesign` for auditing and upgrading an existing UI
- `motion-director` when motion becomes a major part of the solution
